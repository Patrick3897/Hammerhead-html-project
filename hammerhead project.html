The hammerhead shark

About hammerhead sharks

The great hammerhead is a solitary, nomadic predator and is found throughout the Atlantic and Indian Oceans and Pacific oceans and also in the Mediterranean sea.

Learn More

Species

  • Smooth hammerhead
  • Great hammerhead
  • Winghead hammerhead

Features

Most hammerhead sharks are grayish or brownish. Though, the smooth hammerhead shark is olive green or dark gray-brown on the top and white underneath.

Seas with Small Hammerhead Populations

some seas that hammerhead sharks don't populate in are the Banda sea, Arafura sea, Coral sea and the Tasman sea. This is because Hammerhead sharks or any other type of shark species typically dont look for prey in southern oceans. Hammerhead sharks mostly like water above 60 feet but can be found in much deeper areas.
